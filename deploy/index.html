As you enter the Show, groves of Citrus trees lead the way providing a lush dramatic promenade to the sunshine drenched landscape ahead. Drifts of purple and white spiked salvia, specimen succulents and, an intoxicating variety of scented geraniums, roses, rosemary and sage create a stunning mosaic that is at once picturesque and charming. Walk the esplanade of flowering arches.

Rivera Holiday beckons you to embrace your inner romantic and create a Mediterranean inspired garden of your own. Ornate pottery and patterned tiles, a well-placed pergola and abundant clusters of scented flowers, ornamental fountains and herb parterres provide irresistible appeal along with sustainable lower maintenance, water-wise options that are both responsive to and reflective of temperate conditions.

Explore the allure and extraordinary plant palette of some of the world’s captivating coastal regions.

History

The PHS Philadelphia Flower Show is the nation's largest and longest-running horticultural event and features stunning displays by the world’s premier floral and landscape designers.

Started in 1829 by the Pennsylvania Horticultural Society, the Show introduces the newest plant varieties, garden and design concepts, and organic and sustainable practices.

In addition to the major garden displays, the Flower Show hosts world-renowned competitions in horticulture and artistic floral arranging, gardening presentations and demonstrations, special events, a mammoth indoor Marketplace, and the Bloom Philly Festival in the weeks leading up to the Show.

A Royal History

Show organizers wanted to invite a special guest for the 1976 show held during the year of the nation’s bicentennial and were delighted when Princess Grace of Monaco accepted their invitation. Princess Grace, a Philadelphia native, was a practitioner of the floral arts and studied the art of pressed flower compositions from Katie King, a long-time show competitor. Photo courtesy PHS McLean Library & Archives. Proceeds from the Flower Show benefit the year-round programs of PHS that have become national models of urban greening.

Pennsylvania Horticultural Society

PHS is a diverse community of people and organizations who believe in the power of horticulture to make positive social and environmental change. This community invests their time, passion, money, and gardens in our cause. Together, we produce the Philadelphia Flower Show, offer a network of free-to-all public gardens and landscapes, educate and inspire home gardeners, and work with residents in 250+ neighborhoods to plant trees, tend community gardens and vacant lots, and help people transition from incarceration to stable employment.

PHS uses horticulture to advance the health and well-being of the Greater Philadelphia region. We accomplish this goal by increasing access to fresh food, promoting healthy living environments, fostering deep social connections, and creating economic opportunities.
